    • Highlight(pink) - Page 168 ¬∑ Location 3482 Nietzsche was a linguist and philologist before he became a philosopher, and his philosophy starts from linguistic analysis. He was one of the first‚Äîafter the enigmatic Giambattista Vico‚Äîto observe that linguistic grids mold perception and constrain thought. His scandalous, hilarious and dangerous (he loved the concept) criticism of conventional morals grew directly out of this and was largely a criticism of neurosemantic habits, of how words hypnotize us and predetermine hasty Verdicts. Eventually, he asked himself: what if linguistic grids are only in our heads and not “out there”? What if etic experience, existence itself, not edited by the brain’s emic card-index system, is formless, or multiform, or perpetually evolving‚Äîtoo fluid and “dancing” to be captured in one model or one linguistic reality-tunnel?
